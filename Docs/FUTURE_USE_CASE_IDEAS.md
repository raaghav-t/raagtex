# Creative Use-Case Ideas

These are forward-looking ideas that stay aligned with local-first workflows and avoid generic cloud-collab patterns.

## 1) Compile Confidence Trail
After each compile, generate a short "what changed" card:
- page count deltas
- newly introduced warnings/errors
- changed labels/references/bibliography state

Why it matters: users quickly see if a compile materially changed output quality, not just pass/fail.

## 2) Thesis Milestone Mode
A long-form writing mode optimized for chapter-based projects:
- chapter-level compile target shortcuts
- unresolved cross-reference tracker per chapter
- timeline of compile health across drafting sessions

Why it matters: thesis users often need structured progress more than IDE complexity.

## 3) Figure-Safety Watch
Detect when figure/table dimensions repeatedly cause overfull boxes or float churn and surface remediation hints.

Why it matters: layout instability is one of the most frustrating recurring LaTeX pain points.

## 4) Citation Integrity Monitor
Track bib changes and citation fallout:
- unresolved citation burst alerts
- stale bibliography run detection
- quick summary of newly uncited or missing keys

Why it matters: this reduces invisible bibliography regressions in publication workflows.

## 5) Proof Packet Export
One-click local export bundle for advisor/reviewer feedback:
- compiled PDF
- selected diagnostics summary
- optional page-change digest between two compile snapshots

Why it matters: creates a lightweight review handoff without needing online document platforms.
